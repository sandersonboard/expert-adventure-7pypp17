Fixed Assets Process Narrative
Location: Los Angeles - America
Mega Process: Fixed Assets

Processes included:
  • Acquisition
  • Accounting
  • Master data changes
  • Physical Existence
  • Disposals
Systems included:
  • DCW: Accounting general ledger system. The system is interfaced with the ERP application to automatically record purchasing, sales and inventory transactions. Other transactions are manually recorded to DCW.
  • AS400: ERP system. The system is the source application for sales, purchasing and inventory transactions.

The report is considered to be IPE since it is information prepared by the entity (Retail Finance Department) and used in the operation of a relevant control.

CAPITAL BUDGET

#US.FA.1.C01: Capital Expenditure is approved.

Copies of all fixed asset invoices are given to the Fixed Asset Accountant to add into the SAP Fixed Asset System. For asset acquisitions, the final approval comes from the Department VP and the CFO, significant commitments in excess of 500,000 USD will be approved by the Global CFO in addition to the Department VP and Americas CFO. Asset disposals are approved by the respective Department Head. Capital project forms for expenditures at or above the $100k threshold are completed and approved per the DOA policy. The project number is to be included on all invoices when submitted to accounts payable. Department managers submit capital project requests using the paper-based capital project request form. Accounting manually enters the request into SAP and assigns the Internal Order Number (I/O).

PROCESSING FIXED ASSET INVOICES

Upon receipt of a vendor invoice, accounts payable date stamps the invoice and forwards it to the appropriate department for approval and coding. The invoice is coded with the proper general ledger account number and capital project number. The general ledger coding is checked by the Fixed Asset Accountant upon receiving notification.

Design Quality Deficient
Redesign Recommended
Last analyzed: 6/27/2026, 10:16:44 AM
✕ Specificity

The control specifies a quarterly cadence and a spend threshold, but does not define the review steps, what constitutes 'appropriate' managers, or the criteria for manager acknowledgement.

✕ Responsible Party

'The Accountant' is a named role and sufficiently specific, but 'appropriate managers' is vague and does not specify named roles or titles for conducting the review.

✕ Evidence & Testability

Sent emails and manager acknowledgements are described as retained evidence, but the mechanism is informal and there is no specification of how discrepancies are identified or addressed, so completion is difficult to test.

✕ Precision

A dollar threshold is given, but there are no criteria for what managers are to review, how to identify duplicate or missing Internal Order numbers, or what to do if errors are found.

✕ Risk Coverage

The control only requests manager acknowledgement of high-spend projects without procedures to validate uniqueness or existence of Internal Order numbers or detect misclassification, so it does not directly address the risk of interface errors resulting in duplicate or missing project identifiers.
