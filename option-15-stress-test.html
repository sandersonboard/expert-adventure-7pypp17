How brittleness is scored

For each estimate, we run N=2,000 Latin-hypercube perturbation trials across its 3–5 drivers and record the smallest single-driver move that flips the audit conclusion. The brittleness score is that minimum, expressed in percentage points (0 = instant flip, 50 = effectively unmovable).

Brittlenessi = minj ∈ drivers |Δj| such that
  Conclusion(baseline ⊕ Δj · ej) ≠ Conclusion(baseline)

A score under 5 pp means a 1 pp shift in any single assumption flips sufficiency — well inside an auditor's normal challenge band. At N=2,000 the percentile estimate carries a 95% CI of ±0.7 pp, comfortably tighter than the 5 pp band width.

  • Brittle < 5 ppflips inside the auditor's typical ±5 pp range
  • Fragile 5–15 ppflips within reasonable challenge
  • Elastic 15–25 ppflips only on a sizeable move
  • Stable > 25 ppholds past any reasonable challenge

Rows are sorted ascending by brittleness; $ exposure is the secondary sort so two equally-brittle estimates queue largest-dollar first. Sampling design follows AICPA AU-C 540 (accounting estimates) and the Latin-hypercube guidance in Helton & Davis (2003); the ±0.7 pp CI is computed per Cochran (1977), §4.3.
